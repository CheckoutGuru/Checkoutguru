Products — Frequently Asked Questions

How do I know if my Shopee product is legit?

  • Check the seller: prefer Official Stores, Shopee Mall, or high-rated sellers (rating ≥ 4.8).
  • Read recent reviews with photos and videos — they show actual items and packaging.
  • Compare product details (model numbers, serials, logos) with the manufacturer website.
  • Watch for warranties, receipts, and genuine packaging; verify serials with the brand if possible.

How to detect scammer in Shopee?

  • Red flags: extremely low prices, no reviews, new seller with many different product types, stock photos copied across listings.
  • Avoid sellers asking to pay outside Shopee — always use the platform's checkout and payment methods.
  • Check seller response time and the content of replies; evasive or inconsistent answers are suspicious.

Is Shopee 100% authentic real?

No. Shopee is a marketplace hosting many sellers. Many items are genuine, but third-party sellers can offer counterfeits — use Official Stores and Shopee Mall for stronger guarantees.

Why are products cheaper in Shopee?

  • Marketplace competition and promotions lower prices.
  • Some low prices come from grey-market or counterfeit goods — very cheap offers can be risky.

Can I get refunded if I got scammed?

Usually yes if you follow Shopee's dispute process: open a case within the buyer protection window, provide evidence (photos, messages), and request a refund. Shopee's Guarantee holds payment until delivery confirmation for eligible purchases.

How to know if a seller is scamming?

  • Look for: inconsistent listing details, mismatched images, pressure to buy quickly, unusual payment requests, very low ratings or none at all.
  • Check other listings by the seller — many unrelated products or frequent relistings are suspicious.

Are there fake products on Shopee?

Yes. Counterfeit goods exist on most large marketplaces. Use seller verification, reviews, and Shopee Mall to reduce risk.

How to check if a product is real or fake?

  1. Compare product photos, logos and specs with the brand's official site.
  2. Read at least 10 recent reviews that include photos — beware of many identical short positive reviews.
  3. Confirm seller history and ratings, and look for warranty/return policies.
  4. When received, inspect packaging, serial numbers and build quality; test features and compare to known genuine units.
  5. If unsure, contact the brand with the serial number or request an authenticity check.
